# Interview Preparation Overview

Welcome to the DevOps Interview Preparation guide. This section provides comprehensive interview questions and resources for engineers targeting senior and staff-level positions in DevOps, SRE, and Platform Engineering roles.

## How to Use These Materials

This interview prep section is structured to mirror how real interviews are conducted. Each technology track follows a similar pattern:

1. **Conceptual Questions** — Test your understanding of fundamentals and deep internals
2. **Practical / Hands-On Questions** — Verify you can actually do the work, not just talk about it
3. **Troubleshooting Questions** — Assess your diagnostic and debugging methodology
4. **Architecture / Design Questions** — Evaluate your system design thinking
5. **Security Questions** — Probe your security mindset and common vulnerabilities
6. **Scenario-Based Questions** — Real production scenarios testing multi-technology integration

## What Senior/Staff Engineers Demonstrate

Beyond answering questions correctly, interviewers are evaluating:

### Technical Depth Over Breadth
- You understand **why** something works, not just **what** it does
- You can explain trade-offs and alternative approaches
- You have battle-tested experience from production incidents
- You know the limits and failure modes of technologies you use

### Problem-Solving Methodology
- You ask clarifying questions before jumping to solutions
- You systematically isolate variables during debugging
- You think in terms of probability and prioritize accordingly
- You know when to escalate and when to dig deeper yourself

### Communication Skills
- You can explain complex concepts to mixed audiences
- You structure answers logically with clear assumptions
- You acknowledge uncertainty honestly
- You follow up questions to confirm understanding

### Leadership and Influence
- You mention mentoring and teaching others
- You have opinions on engineering culture and practices
- You can discuss making trade-offs that benefit the team long-term
- You've driven technical initiatives across teams

## Common Interview Formats

### Screen Interviews (45-60 minutes)
- Usually a single interviewer
- Focus on fundamentals and past experience
- May include live coding or shell exercises
- **Tip**: These are often filter rounds — don't over-engineer answers

### Technical Deep Dives (60-75 minutes)
- Deep dive into 2-3 topics
- Expect whiteboard or live system debugging
- Scenario-based questions
- **Tip**: Talk through your thinking — interviewers want to see your process

### System Design Interviews (45-60 minutes)
- Design a distributed system end-to-end
- Emphasis on trade-offs and scale considerations
- Not looking for "the right answer" — multiple valid approaches
- **Tip**: Start with requirements clarification and constraints

### Behavioral Interviews (45-60 minutes)
- Past experience using STAR format (Situation, Task, Action, Result)
- Questions about conflict resolution, failure, and growth
- Cultural fit and team dynamics
- **Tip**: Prepare 5-7 strong stories with metrics and impact

### Take-Home Projects (4-8 hours)
- Real-world style problem
- Focus on production-quality code
- Documentation and testing matter
- **Tip**: Scope creep is your enemy — deliver something complete, not something half-done

## Interview Stages by Company Type

### FAANG/Mega-Corp
- Heavy focus on data structures and algorithms for some roles
- Multiple rounds with different interviewers
- Well-defined rubrics and calibration processes
- **Expect**: LC-hard problems for some roles, system design for others

### Startups (Series B+)
- More practical, less academic
- Will ask about your specific stack
- Expect you to discuss trade-offs based on experience
- **Expect**: Practical system design, incident analysis

### Traditional Enterprises
- May focus more on process and methodology
- Less emphasis on cutting-edge technology
- Want to see稳定性和可靠性
- **Expect**: Process-heavy questions, change management

### Pure SRE Roles (Google, Netflix, etc.)
- Heavy focus on debugging and simulation
- Expect you to work in Linux shell environment
- Deep questions on networking, storage, kernel
- **Expect**: Live troubleshooting, on-call scenarios

## Study Strategy

### Week 1-2: Foundation Assessment
- Take 2-3 real interviews to identify gaps
- Review fundamentals you use daily but may have forgotten
- Focus on areas where you stumbled or hesitated

### Week 3-4: Deep Dives
- Pick 2-3 technologies to master thoroughly
- Read source code or deep blog posts on internals
- Build something to verify understanding

### Week 5-6: Integration and Scenarios
- Practice multi-technology scenarios
- Focus on trade-offs and decision-making
- Mock interviews with peers or coaches

### Week 7+: Maintenance and Polish
- Light review of all topics
- Practice articulating your past experience
- Focus on communication and clarity

## Red Flags to Avoid

- **Vague hand-waving** — "It just works" is not an answer
- **Memorization without understanding** — Can you explain *why*?
- **Ignoring constraints** — Always ask about scale, latency, and reliability requirements
- **Dismissive of alternatives** — Good engineers acknowledge trade-offs
- **Blame-shifting** — Own your failures and explain what you learned

## Questions You Should Ask

Interviews are bidirectional. Prepare questions that reveal:

- Engineering culture and team dynamics
- On-call expectations and rotation
- Deployment frequency and methodology
- How incidents are handled post-mortem
- Opportunities for learning and growth
